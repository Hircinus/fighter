Basic idea

You are the fighter. They are the enemy. That's the objective.
Beat as many enemies as you can (with some strategy, of course) before losing all your health.

Deeper look into the mechanics

During your encounters, you have four available options to choose from:

  1. Sword
  2. Shield
  3. Blaster
  4. Heal
Please bear in mind that this game is not yet fully complete, but it is functional.

Sword

The sword ability deals 20 damage to foes and takes away 10 energy points from you.

Shield

The shield ability recovers 5 energy points for you and quarters the damage your enemy inflicts while you shield.

Blaster

The blaster ability deals 25 damage to foes and takes away 30 energy points from you.
However, when using the blaster, your enemy's attack damage is halved.

Heal

The heal ability recovers 5 health for you but takes 10 energy. Enemies cannot hurt you while you heal.

Detailed game mechanics and calculations

Player health

Player health is set at the beginning of the game to "100". It will decrease from enemies' attacks until it reaches zero, which is game over.
After each defeated foe, you regain 40 health (to a maximum of 100).

Player energy

Player energy, like health, is set to "100" at the beginning. It decreases with every ability the player uses (except shield, which recovers energy).
Without energy, the player cannot do any action except shield.
After each defeated foe, you regain 20 energy (to a maximum of 100).

Enemy level

The foe's level ("lvl.") is determined using a random number generator that creates an integer between 1 and 10.
Many other calculations rely on this determined number, which changes after each time you defeat the previous enemy.

Enemy attack damage

The enemy's attack damage is mainly a random integer between 1 and 5, which is then multiplied by the foe's level.
The other elements that affect this are the blaster and shield which half and quarter this value.

Enemy accuracy

The enemy's accuracy is a fixed percentage of 60%. The only exception is with the blaster ability, which decreases it to 40%.

Score

Your score is simply the sum of the levels of all the enemies you defeat.
